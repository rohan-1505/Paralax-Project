Ro-Hitman Sharma

Rohit Sharma is an Indian cricketer, widely regarded as one of the most talented and successful batsmen in modern cricket. Known for his elegant strokeplay and ability to play under pressure, he has achieved numerous records and accolades in international cricket.

Previously, he also captained the team in Twenty20 International (T20I) matches and led India's win in 2024 ICC Men's T20 World Cup, subsequent to which he retired from T20s in June 2024. He is considered to be one of the best batsmen of his generation and one of the greatest opening batters of all time

A Historic ODI Knock - 264 Against Sri Lanka

Rohit Sharma's 264 runs against Sri Lanka on November 13, 2014, is a record-breaking achievement that stands tall in the history of One-Day International (ODI) cricket. It is the highest individual score in an ODI match to date, and the performance was remarkable for its consistency and flair.

Record:

  • Rohit Sharma scored 264 runs from 173 balls.
  • It remains the highest individual score in the history of ODI cricket.
  • He surpassed his own record of 209 runs, which he had set earlier against Australia.
  • He hit 33 fours and 9 sixes, showcasing his power and precision with the bat.
  • This knock helped India to a massive total and was instrumental in India's victory in that match.

A Historic ODI Knock--

Rohit Sharma's 208 runs against Australia on November 2, 2013, was a game-changing performance in the history of One-Day International (ODI) cricket. It was, at the time, the second-highest individual score in ODI history, and it remains one of the most incredible knocks by any batsman. Record:

  • Rohit Sharma scored 208 runs from 153 balls.
  • It was the highest individual score by an Indian batsman in ODIs at the time.
  • He hit 12 fours and 16 sixes during his innings.
  • Rohit broke his own previous record of 209 runs, set in 2013 against Australia, before eventually scoring his 264 against Sri Lanka.

A Historic ODI Knock - 209 Against Sri Lanka

Rohit Sharma's 209 runs against Sri Lanka on November 2, 2013, was a record-breaking achievement that announced his arrival as one of the premier batsmen in world cricket. This innings not only helped India secure a dominant position in the match but also set the record for the highest individual score by an Indian in ODIs at the time.

Record:

  • Rohit Sharma scored 209 runs from 158 balls.
  • It was the first-ever double century by an Indian batsman in ODI cricket.
  • He became the third player in ODI history to score more than 200 runs in a single inning.
  • He hit 12 fours and 16 sixes, showcasing both his power and timing.
  • Rohit broke the record of 194 runs held by **Virender Sehwag** to become the highest individual scorer for India at that time.
